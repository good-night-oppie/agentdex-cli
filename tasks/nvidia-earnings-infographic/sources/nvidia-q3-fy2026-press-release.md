Issued November 19, 2025. Quarter ended October 26, 2025. Source: NVIDIA Investor Relations + SEC Form 8-K.

## Headline numbers

- Revenue: $57.0 billion (up 22% QoQ, up 62% YoY)
- GAAP gross margin: 73.4% (sequential improvement ~0.9pp from Q2 FY2026)
- Non-GAAP gross margin: 73.6%
- GAAP EPS: $1.30 diluted
- Non-GAAP EPS: $1.30 diluted
- Net income: $31.91 billion

## Segment breakdown

| Segment | Q3 FY2026 Revenue | QoQ | YoY |
|---|---|---|---|
| Data Center | $51.21 billion | +25% | +66% |
| Gaming | $4.30 billion | -1% | +30% |
| Professional Visualization | $0.76 billion | +26% | +56% |
| Automotive | $0.59 billion | +1% | +32% |
| OEM & Other | $0.17 billion | n/a | +79% |

## Data Center sub-segments

- Data Center compute revenue: $43.03 billion (+56% YoY); Blackwell + Blackwell Ultra adopted by all major hyperscaler, enterprise, sovereign, and industrial customers
- Networking revenue: $8.19 billion (+162% YoY); NVLink compute fabric + GB200/GB300 rack-scale shipments driving the growth
- H20 sales were insignificant in Q3; export-control posture unchanged

## Guidance for Q4 FY2026

- Revenue: $65.0 billion ± 2%
- GAAP gross margin: 74.8% ± 50 bps
- Non-GAAP gross margin: 75.0% ± 50 bps
- Operating expenses GAAP: ~$6.7 billion
- Operating expenses non-GAAP: ~$5.0 billion
- Tax rate: 17%
- Outlook assumes zero data center compute contribution from China

## Capital return

- Quarterly cash dividend: $0.01 per share, payable December 26, 2025
- Share repurchases continue per board authorization

## Product family commentary

- Blackwell Ultra is the leading architecture across all customer categories; prior Blackwell generation still in continued strong demand
- Blackwell shipment pace: 1,000+ racks per week, reflecting global AI deployment acceleration
- Rubin platform succession sequence on track; CFO cited a $500B opportunity for Blackwell + Rubin platforms through CY2026

## Forward statements

This release contains forward-looking statements regarding Q4 FY2026 guidance, Blackwell + Blackwell Ultra ramp, Rubin platform readiness, and AI demand. Actual results may differ materially.
